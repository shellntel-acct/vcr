Introduction

Firstly, thank you for choosing SynerComm. We truly do appreciate the opportunity to serve your organization. This report contains the results of a recent vulnerability scan which was run against your systems, and contains sensitive data which should be treated as highly confidential.

SynerComm uses best of breed tools and techniques in order to assess your organization's vulnerabilities. One of these utilities is the Nessus Vulnerability Scanner, one of the most well known and widely used vulnerability scanners in the world. Nessus scans provide an indepth look at a target network's vulnerabilities, allowing an organization to effectively understand and prioritize remediation efforts.

Details

Nessus breaks down all vulnerabilities into one of 5 categories: Critical, High, Medium, Low, and Informational. These categories are based on the Common Vulnerability Scoring System (CVSS). A presentation detailing the CVSS 2.0 standard can be found here.

The below table describes each classification.

Critical Exploitation of the vulnerability is straightforward and widely known to attackers, potentially resulting in administrative or root level privileges. Unless other mitigating controls are in place, it is advised to patch or upgrade these vulnerabilities as soon as possible.
High High-severity vulnerabilities are widely known to attackers, but may be difficult to exploit. Successful exploitation may not immediately result in administrative privileges or sensitive data loss.
Medium A vulnerability with a "Medium" classification poses difficulty to an attacker, often requiring the attacker and victim to be on the same local network. Medium severity vulnerabilities may also affect obscure applications or configurations, with successful exploitation providing very limited access.
Low Low severity vulnerabilities typically have little impact on an organization's business or operations. Generally Low severity vulnerabilities are the result of simple misconfigurations that are easily corrected. Exploitation does not result in unauthorized access or data loss.
Informational The Informational category describes information that may be useful, however not related to a vulnerability. An example is an informational message describing the open ports found on a system. Informational messages are not classified as vulnerabilities and do not affect the percentages on the "At-A-Glance" chart on the left.
